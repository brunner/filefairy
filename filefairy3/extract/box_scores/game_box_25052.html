Mariners Even With Dodgers After Home Win
The Seattle Mariners defeated the Los Angeles Dodgers, 3-2 in Game 4 of the World Series today. Earning player of the game honors was starter Kikaku Ando, who threw 7 innings of 4-hit ball. The win moves Seattle into a 2-2 tie with Los Angeles in the series.

Had Trent Clark not delivered a key base hit in the bottom of the eighth, the outcome might have been different. Instead, with two down and the bases empty, Clark hit a solo home run. That made the score 3-2, in favor of the Mariners.

"If you want to be considered a good team, these are the kind of games you have to win," said Seattle manager dotalitarian Smith.

The next game is scheduled for Wednesday, November 2nd, 2022 at Safeco Field. Slated to start are Mario Ramírez for the Dodgers and Ashe Russell for the Mariners.
