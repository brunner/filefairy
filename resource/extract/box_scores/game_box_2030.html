Betts Excels in Texas Win
The skies opened up and the rain fell at Globe Life Park in Arlington.

The Texas Rangers knocked off the Houston Astros, 8-2, at Globe Life Park in Arlington. Chris Betts, who captured player of the game honors, was a big reason Texas won. The catcher went 3 for 5 with a home run and 2 doubles. He drove in 5 runs and scored once. The Rangers now own a record of 53-43.

Betts had a game-changing home run in the bottom of the seventh. On a full count fastball from Sonny Gray, he banged a grand slam home run to center, giving the Rangers a 6-2 lead. It was the 12th home run of the year for Betts.

In postgame comments to the Texas Times, Betts said, "If the effort is there, the wins will come."
