The Colorado Rockies defeated the Cincinnati Reds, 11-7 in Game 4 of the League Championship Series today. Earning player of the game honors was first baseman José Uribe, who belted 2 home runs. He drove in 4 runs and scored twice. The win moves Colorado into a 2-2 tie with Cincinnati in the series.

Uribe had a consequential at-bat in the bottom of the first for the Rockies. Coming up with a runner on 2nd and Colorado trailing 1-0, he hit a 2-run home run.

"The name of the game is scoring runs," Uribe said. "We did a good job of that today."

The next game is set to be played tomorrow. Slated to start are Ken Richards for the Cincinnati Reds and Joe Burris for the the Colorado Rockies.
