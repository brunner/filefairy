Williams Hits 2 home runs in White Sox Win
It was a fun time at U.S. Cellular Field, where the New York Mets and Chicago White Sox tangled.

The Chicago White Sox beat the New York Mets, 11-4, at U.S. Cellular Field. The win was largely due to the efforts of left fielder Justin Williams, who captured player of the game honors. He went 2-3 with 2 home runs, a walk, and was hit by a pitch. He scored 3 runs and drove in 4. Zack Wheeler got the save, the 1st for him this season.

Williams had an important at-bat with runners on 1st and 2nd in the bottom of the third, when he hit a 3-run home run. The White Sox went on to win.

"Win or lose, we pick each other up in this clubhouse," Williams told the Chicago Bulletin.

With the win over New York, the White Sox improved to 32-33.
