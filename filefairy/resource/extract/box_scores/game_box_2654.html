Robertson, Rays Rough Up Indians
The Tampa Bay Rays grabbed a 11-3 win over the Cleveland Indians at Tropicana Field. Tampa Bay shortstop Daniel Robertson was 4 for 4 with a home run and 3 singles, while scoring 4 runs and driving in 2 to earn player of the game honors. Jarred Cosart got credit for the win, improving to 5-9. He allowed 3 runs on 7 hits in 7 innings. Jesus A. Sanchez finished to wrap up his 2nd save.

Robertson contributed a big hit, a 2-run home run, for Tampa Bay in the bottom of the second. With his team trailing 1-0, Robertson took a fastball from Robbie Erlin and stroked the home run to left. His 10th home run of the season allowed the Rays to leap in front, 2-1.

"I'm just happy to help the team win," Robertson told the Tampa Bay Gazette.

The Rays' win now puts them at 47-92.
